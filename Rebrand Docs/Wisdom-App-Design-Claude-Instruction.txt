I am building a Christian prayer and meditation web app called Wilderness. Use the attached design board as the primary visual reference. Please revamp the current build to match this aesthetic while keeping the app simple, calm, responsive, and mobile-first.

Brand identity

App name:
Wilderness

Tagline:
Beyond the noise, transformation begins.

Clarifying line:
Slow down with God right where you are.

Brand promise:
Wilderness helps people slow down, step away from distraction, and make space for God’s transforming work in the middle of everyday life.

Core positioning:
Wilderness is not about leaving your life behind. It is about slowing down enough to meet God honestly within it. Through guided journeys of Scripture, silence, and prayer, Wilderness helps users make space for transformation one ordinary day at a time.

Important concept:
The user does not need to leave their life to enter the wilderness. The wilderness is not a travel destination, retreat, adventure, or escape. It is a spiritual posture of slowing down, becoming honest before God, practicing silence, receiving Scripture, and returning to prayer in ordinary life.

First program:
Desert

Desert description:
A free 30-day journey through Scripture, silence, and prayer for ordinary life.

Tone:
Quiet, reflective, honest, Christian, emotionally clear, non-hype, peaceful, minimal, and grounded. The app should feel gentle, spacious, and sacred without feeling overly churchy or ornate.

Visual direction

Use the attached image as the visual system reference.

Overall style:
Minimalist, warm, contemplative, modern, soft, earthy, mobile-first.
The design should feel calm and premium but not flashy.
Use generous whitespace, rounded corners, soft shadows, subtle borders, and quiet typography.
The interface should feel like a peaceful prayer journal blended with a simple meditation app.

Color palette:
Use warm off-white and desert-inspired neutral tones.

Suggested palette:
Background ivory: #F6F3EC
Warm sand: #E9E1D2
Soft beige: #D6C8B3
Muted taupe: #8A7A67
Deep charcoal: #2B2A28

Usage:
Use #F6F3EC as the main page background.
Use #E9E1D2 and #D6C8B3 for cards, chips, dividers, and warm accents.
Use #8A7A67 for secondary text, borders, icons, and muted emphasis.
Use #2B2A28 for primary text and primary buttons.
Use white or near-white cards sparingly for contrast.
Avoid bright colors, gradients that feel techy, harsh blacks, saturated blues, or neon accents.

Typography:
Use a refined serif for major headings and a clean sans-serif for body text.

Preferred type pairing:
Headings: Playfair Display or Cormorant Garamond
Body: Inter or similar clean sans-serif

Heading style:
Large, elegant, calm, high contrast serif.
Use sentence case, not all caps, except for small labels.

Body style:
Clean, readable, soft, and spacious.
Use comfortable line height.
Avoid dense blocks of text.

Small labels:
Use uppercase with letter spacing for labels like CURRENT PROGRAM, WILDERNESS, DESERT, SCRIPTURE, SILENCE, PRAYER.

Layout principles

Mobile-first:
The app will primarily be used on phones. Prioritize a beautiful mobile layout first, then adapt to desktop.

Spacing:
Use generous padding throughout.
Cards should have breathing room.
Avoid cramped layouts.
Avoid too much text visible at once.

Cards:
Rounded corners between 20px and 28px.
Soft shadow or subtle border.
Warm translucent or near-white card backgrounds.
Cards should feel soft, not stark.

Buttons:
Primary buttons should be dark charcoal with white text.
Large rounded pill style.
Secondary buttons should have a warm border and transparent or ivory background.
Disabled buttons should be warm beige or muted taupe with low contrast.

Icons:
Use simple thin-line icons.
Use icons for Home, Calendar, Profile, Book, Bell, Leaf, Clock, Pencil, More, Settings.
Icons should be minimal and quiet.
Do not use heavy filled icon styles except when indicating active state.

Imagery:
Use abstract desert dunes as a soft visual metaphor, but avoid making the app feel like travel or adventure.
Dunes should be subtle, warm, blurred, and used as background texture.
The desert metaphor should feel spiritual and contemplative, not outdoorsy.
No face-led imagery.
No people-focused imagery.
No heroic landscapes.
No camping, hiking, mountains, or adventure visuals.

Logo direction:
Simple Wilderness wordmark.
Optional circular mark with a W and abstract layered desert lines.
Keep logo understated.
Do not overuse the logo.
